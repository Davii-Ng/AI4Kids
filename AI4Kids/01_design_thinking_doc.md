# Design Thinking Document: AI4Kids

## Product Thesis

AI4Kids is the "AI driving school" for children ages 10-14. It does not replace a teacher or provide unrestricted companionship. It gives schools a safe, teacher-ready way to let students practice with AI while proving that they still did the thinking.

**Beachhead market:** Private and bilingual schools, English/STEM centers, and after-school programs in urban Vietnam.

**Economic buyer:** School owner, principal, academic director, or center manager.

**Daily users:** Students and teachers.

**Trust buyer:** Parents.

## Empathize

### Student

Students already encounter chatbots for homework, search, creativity, and social interaction. They want speed and independence, but may:

- Treat fluent output as fact.
- Share names, schools, locations, photos, or personal problems.
- Ask AI to do the difficult thinking instead of supporting it.
- Confuse simulated empathy with human understanding.
- Hide AI use because classroom rules are unclear.

### Teacher

Teachers are pressured to adopt AI but lack time, confidence, and policy clarity. The research reports that 64% of surveyed Vietnamese teachers identify policy ambiguity as a barrier, 70.5% cite limited technical competence, and 60.8% report infrastructure constraints.

Their urgent job is not "teach another technology course." It is:

> Let students use AI productively without creating cheating, privacy, or classroom-management problems.

### Parent

Parents see possible educational value but fear dependency, job displacement, unsafe content, and loss of independent thought. They need evidence of healthy habits, not more screen-time data or private chat surveillance.

### School Leader

School leaders want to demonstrate innovation to families and regulators while reducing institutional risk. They need a consistent policy, teacher onboarding, measurable outcomes, and a program that works on shared or low-cost devices.

## Define

### Core Problem

Vietnamese schools and learning centers lack a practical operating system for student AI use: clear rules, short curriculum, safe practice, teacher evidence, and parent communication.

### Jobs To Be Done

- **School leader:** "Help me approve and communicate responsible AI use without creating a policy and curriculum from scratch."
- **Teacher:** "Help me run a useful AI activity in 20 minutes and see whether students thought critically."
- **Student:** "Help me use AI for a real task without getting tricked, oversharing, or losing ownership of my work."
- **Parent:** "Show me that my child is becoming more capable, not more dependent."

### How Might We

How might we make responsible AI use easy to teach, visible to adults, and rewarding to children without monitoring private conversations or becoming another general-purpose tutor?

## Ideate

### Selected Product Loop: Think, Ask, Check, Protect, Show

1. **Think:** Student records an initial idea, prediction, or attempted solution before AI appears.
2. **Ask:** Student builds a purposeful prompt using Goal, Context, and Format.
3. **Check:** Student identifies uncertain claims and verifies at least one.
4. **Protect:** Student removes personal data and recognizes high-stakes boundaries.
5. **Show:** Product creates an **AI Use Receipt** showing the learning process, not private chat content.

### High-Value Product Components

- **20-minute Classroom Missions:** Vietnamese and English activities tied to homework, research, creativity, misinformation, and digital citizenship.
- **AI Use Receipt:** A privacy-safe record of the student's initial thinking, AI role, verification action, revision, and final reflection.
- **Teacher Launch Pad:** One-click lesson, facilitation script, printable fallback, and aggregate rubric.
- **School Policy Kit:** Customizable acceptable-use rules, parent letter, consent language, incident flow, and classroom posters.
- **Family Co-Pilot:** A five-minute offline discussion or fact-check challenge after selected missions.
- **AI Use Passport:** A portable skill record across prompting, verification, privacy, independent thinking, and human escalation.

## Why This Wins

AI4Kids does not compete directly with VioEdu on adaptive subject tutoring, ELSA on pronunciation, or Khanmigo on teacher content generation. It is the trust and evidence layer that helps schools use any AI tool responsibly.

Its differentiated promise is:

> Students can use AI, teachers can see the learning process, and parents can trust the outcome.

## Prototype

Build a bilingual, mobile-first prototype for one 20-minute classroom mission:

1. Teacher launches a class code without student email accounts.
2. Student makes a pre-AI attempt.
3. Student completes a short guided AI interaction.
4. Student catches one seeded error and one privacy risk.
5. Student revises the work and identifies the AI's role.
6. Teacher receives class-level rubric results and AI Use Receipts.
7. Parent receives an optional offline discussion prompt.

Use scripted AI responses first. This tests pedagogy, workflow, and willingness to pay without model cost or safety risk.

## Test

### Pilot

- 2-3 private schools or learning centers.
- 6-10 teachers.
- 100-200 students ages 10-14.
- 6-week paid or deposit-backed pilot.

### Critical Questions

- Can a teacher prepare in under 10 minutes and run the mission without specialist support?
- Does the pre-AI attempt reduce answer-copying and improve student ownership?
- Can students explain what AI contributed and what they verified?
- Do AI Use Receipts give teachers useful evidence without becoming grading overhead?
- Will a school pay for the policy kit, curriculum, and outcome report?
- Does the parent-facing proof increase trust in the school?

### Pilot Gates

- At least 80% of students complete the mission.
- At least 70% independently identify a seeded false claim and privacy risk.
- At least 60% improve their final answer after verification.
- Median teacher preparation time is under 10 minutes.
- At least 80% of teachers would run another mission.
- At least 2 of 3 pilot organizations convert or sign a letter of intent.
- Zero critical privacy or child-safety incidents.

## Explicit Non-Goals

- Unrestricted AI companionship.
- Therapy, emotional dependency, or simulated friendship.
- Replacing teachers or parents.
- Maximizing screen time.
- Subject tutoring across the full K-12 curriculum.
- Surveillance of children's private conversations.
