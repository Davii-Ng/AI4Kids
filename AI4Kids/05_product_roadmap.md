# Product Roadmap: AI4Kids

## Vision

Every child learns to use AI as a tool they direct, question, and disclose, never as a substitute for judgment or human connection.

## Positioning

**For:** Vietnamese schools and learning centers that need to permit student AI use safely.

**AI4Kids is:** A responsible AI practice and evidence platform.

**It gives:** Teachers ready-to-run lessons and proof of student thinking.

**Unlike:** General chatbots and subject tutors.

**Why it wins:** It combines Think-before-AI pedagogy, AI Use Receipts, school policy, and parent communication.

## North Star

**Verified Independent Use Rate:** Percentage of active students who make a pre-AI attempt, use AI for a defined role, verify a material claim, revise the work, and accurately disclose AI's contribution.

This metric rewards capability rather than screen time, prompts sent, or answers generated.

## Supporting Metrics

### Learning

- Verification accuracy.
- Privacy-risk recognition.
- Quality of post-AI revision.
- Correct human-escalation choice.
- Retention of skills after four weeks.

### Teacher Value

- Preparation time per mission.
- Percentage of teachers running a second mission.
- Percentage of receipts requiring manual review.
- Teacher-reported confidence and usefulness.

### Business

- Qualified lead to pilot conversion.
- Paid pilot rate.
- Pilot to annual conversion.
- Annual contract value and gross margin.
- Organization renewal and campus expansion.

### Trust And Safety

- Critical incidents.
- Sensitive data retained.
- Parent complaints.
- Time to resolve reports.
- Percentage of students who understand that AI is not a person.

## Phase 0: Problem And Price Validation

**Timing:** Weeks 0-4

### Outcomes

- Confirm the strongest buying trigger.
- Test whether schools will pay for implementation and evidence.
- Validate language, age band, and teacher workflow.

### Work

- Interview 10 school leaders, 15 teachers, 10 parents, and 20 students.
- Demo a policy kit, AI Use Receipt, and one clickable mission.
- Ask for paid pilot commitments or refundable deposits.
- Test price anchors at USD 1,500, 2,000, and 3,000.
- Select 2-3 design partners.

### Gate

- At least two paid or deposit-backed pilots.
- At least 70% of interviewed teachers rank integrity/process visibility among their top three AI concerns.
- Median teacher accepts a 10-minute preparation budget.

## Phase 1: Scripted Paid Pilot

**Timing:** Weeks 5-12

### Product

- Bilingual teacher and student experience.
- Class-code access.
- Baseline assessment.
- Four scripted classroom missions.
- Think, Ask, Check, Protect, Show workflow.
- AI Use Receipt.
- Teacher class summary.
- School policy and parent kit.
- Printable fallback.

### Mission Sequence

1. **Homework Helper, Not Answer Machine:** Make an attempt and request a hint.
2. **Confidently Wrong:** Catch and verify a false claim.
3. **Private By Default:** Remove identifying information.
4. **Who Should Help?:** Route high-stakes and emotional situations to humans.

### Gate

- 80% student completion.
- 70% seeded-error and privacy-risk detection.
- 60% meaningful revision after checking.
- Under 10 minutes median teacher preparation.
- 80% teacher intent to reuse.
- At least 60% pilot-to-annual conversion.

## Phase 2: Repeatable School Product

**Timing:** Months 4-8

### Product

- 12 missions across two age bands.
- AI Use Passport.
- Teacher assignments and reusable class setup.
- School administrator dashboard.
- Branded outcome reports and certificates.
- Teacher micro-certification.
- Policy-version and parent-communication updates.
- Low-bandwidth caching and improved pair mode.

### Commercial

- Standard annual pricing and minimum contract.
- Onboarding playbook.
- Case study and benchmark report.
- Referral program for school leaders and teacher champions.
- Sales pipeline focused on school groups and center chains.

### Gate

- Five annual customers.
- Above 75% scripted gross margin.
- At least two customers expand to another grade or campus.
- Less than two hours of customer-success labor per school per month.

## Phase 3: Supervised Live AI

**Timing:** Months 9-15, only after prior gates

### Product

- Constrained model gateway for selected missions.
- Teacher-selected mode and approved prompt templates.
- Personal-data detection before transmission.
- Output safety filters and non-human disclosure.
- Source and uncertainty prompts.
- Incident reporting and auditable safety versions.

### Gate

- Red-team pass against defined child-safety scenarios.
- Zero critical incidents in supervised beta.
- Learning outcomes match or exceed scripted mode.
- Model and moderation cost stays below 10% of license revenue.
- Schools show higher renewal intent, not novelty-only engagement.

## Phase 4: Distribution Expansion

**Timing:** Months 16-24

- Expand to public-school and rural programs through sponsors and government partners.
- Add curriculum partnerships and regional localization.
- License teacher training and policy implementation.
- Offer privacy-preserving national or regional AI-literacy benchmarks.
- Evaluate adjacent Southeast Asian markets only after Vietnam retention is proven.

## Stop/Change Rules

- If schools like the mission but will not pay, sell implementation, policy, and teacher training before adding features.
- If teachers do not use receipts, simplify them to three structured signals.
- If students game the pre-AI attempt, use short oral, multiple-choice, or teacher-observed baselines.
- If live AI does not improve learning or renewal, keep it optional.
- If direct-to-parent demand appears, test it through school-sponsored family access rather than paid consumer acquisition.

## What We Will Not Build In The First Year

- A general-purpose homework solver.
- An emotional companion.
- A social feed or student messaging.
- Full curriculum coverage.
- Native applications.
- Automated high-stakes grading.
- Deep school-system integrations before repeatable sales.
