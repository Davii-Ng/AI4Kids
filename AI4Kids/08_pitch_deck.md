# Pitch Deck Outline: AI4Kids

## 1. Title

**AI4Kids: The Responsible AI Practice Layer For Schools**

Students use AI. Teachers see the thinking. Parents trust the outcome.

## 2. The Shift Has Already Happened

- AI use among young people is mainstream, not experimental.
- Academic use is rising quickly.
- Children also encounter AI as search, entertainment, and emotional companionship.
- Schools cannot rely on bans, while open adoption creates safety and integrity risks.

Use the research figures as directional evidence and verify primary sources before external publication.

## 3. Vietnam Has A High-Value Adoption Gap

- Vietnam has roughly 17.5 million K-12 students.
- Families devote an unusually high share of household budgets to education.
- Digital transformation and STEM adoption are national priorities.
- Teacher shortages and large classes make scalable technology attractive.
- Yet surveyed teachers report policy ambiguity, limited AI competence, and infrastructure constraints.

**The market does not only need more AI. It needs a safe operating model for the AI already arriving.**

## 4. The Pain

### School Leader

"I need an AI program that signals innovation without creating cheating, privacy, or parent-risk problems."

### Teacher

"I need a lesson I can run tomorrow and evidence that students still did the thinking."

### Parent

"I want my child ready for an AI future, not dependent on a chatbot."

### Student

"I need clear rules for when AI helps and when it crosses the line."

## 5. The Product

AI4Kids is a bilingual classroom practice and evidence platform for ages 10-14.

- Teacher-ready 20-minute missions.
- Think-before-AI student workflow.
- Guided prompting, verification, privacy, and human escalation.
- AI Use Receipts that show process without exposing private chats.
- School acceptable-use policy and parent communication kit.
- Scripted, low-bandwidth mode first; supervised live AI later.

## 6. The Learning Method

### Think

Make an initial attempt before AI appears.

### Ask

Give AI a defined, limited role.

### Check

Verify important claims and uncertainty.

### Protect

Remove private information and involve humans in high-stakes situations.

### Show

Revise the work and disclose exactly what AI contributed.

## 7. The Defensible Artifact

**AI Use Receipt**

It answers the question teachers and parents actually have:

> Did AI support the child's thinking, or replace it?

The receipt captures structured evidence of attempt, AI role, verification, privacy decision, revision, and disclosure without requiring surveillance of full conversations.

## 9. Beachhead And Go-To-Market

**First customers:** Private and bilingual schools, English/STEM centers, and school groups in Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City.

**Sales motion**

1. Lead with a cheating, parent-trust, or AI-readiness diagnostic.
2. Sell a 6-week Responsible AI Readiness Pilot.
3. Deliver four missions, teacher onboarding, policy kit, and outcome report.
4. Convert to an annual per-student license.
5. Expand across grades and campuses through teacher champions.

Public and rural distribution follows through sponsors and institutional partners after repeatable private-market sales.

## 10. Business Model

### Paid Pilot

- Price hypothesis: USD 1,500-3,000 per organization.
- Credited toward annual contract.
- Includes baseline, four missions, onboarding, policy kit, and outcome report.

### Annual License

- Price hypothesis: USD 8-15 per active student per year, subject to a minimum contract.
- Premium parent workshops, teacher certification, custom localization, and safety review.

### Attractive Economics

- Scripted mode minimizes model and moderation cost.
- One institutional sale reaches hundreds of students.
- Policy updates, benchmark reports, and new missions support renewal.
- No advertising or sale of learner data.

## 11. Why Now

- Student AI behavior is moving faster than school policy.
- Parent optimism and anxiety coexist, creating demand for visible responsible use.
- Schools face pressure to innovate but teachers lack implementation support.
- Existing Vietnamese EdTech leaders validate willingness to adopt AI learning products.
- The regulatory direction globally favors child safeguards, transparency, and parental controls.

## 12. Validation Plan

### Phase 0

- 10 leader, 15 teacher, 10 parent, and 20 student interviews.
- Prototype one end-to-end mission.
- Secure two paid or deposit-backed pilots.

### Six-Week Pilot Gates

- 80% student completion.
- 70% error and privacy-risk detection.
- 60% meaningful revision after checking.
- Under 10 minutes teacher preparation.
- 80% teacher intent to reuse.
- 60% pilot-to-annual conversion target.

## 13. Safety Is Product Architecture

- No emotional companion or simulated friendship.
- Persistent disclosure that AI is software.
- Mandatory pre-AI thinking.
- Minimal, pseudonymous student data.
- No behavioral advertising or model training on learner data.
- Structured human escalation.
- No unrestricted live AI until learning, safety, and margin gates pass.

## 14. Roadmap

1. **0-3 months:** Paid scripted pilot with four missions.
2. **4-8 months:** Repeatable school product, 12 missions, passports, reports, certification.
3. **9-15 months:** Supervised live AI after red-team and commercial gates.
4. **16-24 months:** School-group, public, sponsor, and regional expansion.

## 15. The Ask

- Two design-partner schools or learning-center groups.
- Funding for the scripted pilot product, curriculum localization, and child-safety/legal review.
- Introductions to school leaders, teacher networks, and responsible education sponsors.

**Near-term milestone:** Prove that schools will pay for measurable independent AI use before investing in a general live chatbot.
