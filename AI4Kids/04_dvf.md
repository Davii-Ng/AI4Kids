# DVF Framework: AI4Kids

## Desirable

### Evidence From The Research

- Youth AI adoption is already mainstream, while parent use and confidence lag.
- Parents fear dependency and reduced future opportunity.
- Children can benefit from structured AI learning, but open interaction can reduce conversational initiative and increase anthropomorphism.
- Adult co-regulation improves the quality of younger children's AI interaction.
- Vietnamese teachers want AI's efficiency but face policy ambiguity, limited training, infrastructure gaps, and academic-integrity concerns.

### Pain Point Ranking

| Pain Point | Urgency | Willingness To Pay | AI4Kids Fit |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| School lacks safe, consistent student AI policy | High | High | Very high |
| Teacher cannot see whether AI replaced thinking | High | High | Very high |
| Teacher lacks time and AI confidence | High | Medium-high | Very high |
| Parent fears dependency and unsafe use | High | Indirect | High |
| Student wants better chatbot answers | Medium | Low | Medium |
| Student needs full subject tutoring | High | High | Low; crowded market |

### Desirability Hypothesis

Schools will adopt a program that turns AI from a disciplinary problem into a measurable learning skill, provided it is teacher-light and parent-trustworthy.

### Riskiest Assumption

AI Use Receipts are useful enough to influence school purchase and renewal without feeling like extra student or teacher paperwork.

## Viable

### Business Model

Start B2B2C to avoid expensive direct-to-parent acquisition and to make supervised use the default.

**Entry offer: Responsible AI Readiness Pilot**

- 6 weeks.
- 4 student missions.
- Teacher onboarding.
- School policy and parent communication kit.
- Baseline and outcome report.
- Price hypothesis: USD 1,500-3,000 per organization, credited toward annual purchase.

**Annual school license hypothesis**

- USD 8-15 per active student per year, with a minimum contract.
- Includes curriculum, AI Use Passports, teacher dashboard, policy updates, and support.
- Premium services: parent workshop, custom localization, teacher certification, and safety review.

Prices are test hypotheses, not established demand.

### ROI For The Buyer

- Avoids internal policy and curriculum development.
- Reduces teacher lesson preparation and manual process checking.
- Creates a marketable, parent-facing responsible AI program.
- Reduces reputational risk from unsafe or undisclosed student AI use.
- Produces evidence for accreditation, digital transformation, and school reporting.

### ROI For AI4Kids

- Scripted missions keep inference and moderation costs low.
- One school sale reaches many learners.
- Policy updates and curriculum create annual renewal value.
- Teacher champions support low-cost expansion across grades and campuses.
- The product complements, rather than replaces, existing tutoring platforms.

### Go-To-Market

1. Founder-led sales to 20 private schools and learning-center groups in Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City.
2. Sell a paid pilot tied to cheating, parent trust, or digital transformation.
3. Publish anonymized outcome benchmarks.
4. Expand through school groups, teacher networks, and curriculum partners.
5. Use foundations or corporate sponsors later for public and rural access.

### Viability Gates

- At least 30% of qualified discovery calls progress to a pilot proposal.
- At least 50% of pilots are paid or deposit-backed.
- At least 60% of successful pilots convert to annual contracts.
- Gross margin target above 75% in scripted mode.
- Annual organization renewal target above 85%.
- Teacher support remains below 30 minutes per class per term after onboarding.

## Feasible

### MVP

- Responsive bilingual web app.
- Class-code access with pseudonymous student IDs.
- Structured pre-AI attempt.
- Four scripted missions.
- Rule-based privacy and high-stakes checks.
- AI Use Receipt and class rubric.
- Teacher launch pad.
- School policy and parent communication templates.
- Offline/printable mission mode.

### Deliberately Deferred

- Unrestricted live chatbot.
- Emotional support or companionship.
- Automated interpretation of sensitive free text.
- Full learning-management or student-information-system integration.
- Broad subject tutoring.
- Native mobile apps.

### Key Risks And Controls

| Risk | Control |
|---|---|
| Students anthropomorphize AI | Persistent non-human framing; no avatar relationship mechanics |
| AI replaces thinking | Mandatory pre-AI attempt and post-AI revision |
| Sensitive disclosure | Data minimization, local/scripted mode, pattern warnings |
| Teacher overload | Structured events, no chat review, one-page lesson guides |
| Low connectivity | Cached content, pair mode, printable missions |
| Policy uncertainty | Versioned school policy templates and local legal review |
| Child-safety incident | Clear boundaries, reporting flow, human escalation, audit log |

### Feasibility Gate For Live AI

Live generation is introduced only if:

- Scripted pilots demonstrate learning and paid demand.
- A child-safety red team passes defined thresholds.
- Inputs and outputs can be minimized, filtered, logged, and deleted appropriately.
- Schools choose supervised use and approve parent communication.
- Per-student model cost preserves target gross margin.

## Unique Advantage

The defensible asset is not a chatbot. It is the combination of:

- A culturally localized responsible-AI curriculum.
- The Think-before-AI learning pattern.
- AI Use Receipts that make student process auditable without surveillance.
- School policy, teacher workflow, and parent trust in one implementation package.
- Longitudinal, privacy-preserving benchmarks for independent AI use.

## DVF Decision

Proceed with a paid, scripted, Vietnam-first school pilot. Do not build a general AI tutor or direct-to-consumer companion. Treat conversion, teacher time saved, and demonstrated independent thinking as the first proof points.
